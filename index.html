personas & storyboards
- airfryer interface


exploring interactions with a specific interface to understand
the development of user experiences

context

An air fryer is essentially a countertop convection oven that stimulates the process of deep frying without actually using oil to fry the food. In these past few years, air fryers have become a popular healthy alternative to oil frying and convection ovens. Along with their convenient usability, their miniature, transferable design is also garnering attention and makes them popular among people today.


problem

Since air fryers tend to be smaller in size than conventional ovens, I wondered how they were able to balance their functionality with aesthetics.

To explore that, I decided to take a closer look at the air fryer I have at my house to assess the experiences users have with this air fryer. For my air fryer specifically, the interface allows users to intuitively use the two knobs to cook food in the air fryer without complex steps.

Its temperature knob allows the user to adjust the temperature prior to and during cooking. Its timer knob allows for the user to set the duration the user wants the food to be cooked and will stop cooking and make a “Ding” sound once the timer stops. To start air frying, turn the temperature knob to desired temperature, then turn the timer to the amount to time to be cooked.

steps

Step one: Preparation


In order to better assess the problem, I started by creating a sketch of the components of the interface. Here, the main components I looked at were the:

1. Temperature dial knob that adjusts the heat levels/temperature inside the airfryer

2. Time dial knob to adjust the cooking times

3. Power light and fan light

I will be observing what roles these main components play in the experience of the user.

 

Step two: User Observations


To better understand the product interface from the perspective of the users, I made observations about the users' interactions with the interface and interviewed users about their experiences with the interface:

  • How many times do you use the air fryer in a week? What is your purpose for using the air fryer?

    Generally, users note that although they only use the air fryer a few times a week, they find it more convenient than using the oven.

  • Do you ever encounter any trouble navigating the interface?

    Users found that the basic dial knob interface makes it difficult to see the exact temperature and time that it is set to.

  • Does the interface make sense to you? Is it easy to get to what you want?

    Users state that the basic settings help simplify the navigation of the interface so they can quickly get to what they want.

  • Is there anything that would improve your experience with using this air fryer?

    Users note that it has some problems with handling more complicated cooking tasks as it only has one mode/setting for cooking and wishes it would be improved.

  • Finally, would you recommend this air fryer to others?

    Users would recommend this product as it does the job that the interface advertises.

Key Takeaways

Users found the similar visuals of the power and fan button to be confusing and prefers interfaces that account for user errors and are simple to navigate through. In addition, users prefer interfaces that contains presets in order to save time.

reflection

From observing and analyzing the user interactions and experiences with this air fryer and hearing their opinions on the interface, I learned valuable lessons regarding what users typically look for in a household kitchen item.

Firstly, users prefer products that account for/leave room in their interfaces for unexpected user errors. ie. they like to know that if they are confused and make mistakes, they are back to recover from that (instead of having burnt food!).

Secondly, users like to know what is going on during the cooking process. No one likes being kept in the dark, users of air fryers are no different. They like to see what is happening to their food while it is cooking.

Finally, users like products that are fast and simple to use and tend to steer away from products that have too many functions or take too long to cook (so functionality and efficiency go hand in hand!).
